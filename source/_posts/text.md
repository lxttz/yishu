“For salamanders, regeneration after injury, such as the loss of a limb, involves regrowth of structure and restoration of function with the constant possibility of twinning or other odd topographical productions at the site of former injury” (Donna Haraway, 1985). Cyborg's body may also get injured intentionally by a running theme of fear, which goes instability that offline violence implies; lots of traps, insecurity, anxiety, and unknown gazes. In the VR world, when a user is touched by another, the hand controller vibrates, creating a very disorienting and even disturbing physical experience. The first “virtual rape” that happened in Horizon Worlds (a metaverse platform),  indicated that the avatar requires coherent activities to recover from the virtual trauma. 

Inspired by the weird wriggling of geckos before their tails break off when they are in danger, this project expect to mimic this stress response through a series of virtual performances. For 3D modeling technology, the production of model textures is a common and necessary step, which includes the collection and recombination of material maps. In considering ‘scars’, it requires that the texture of the cyborg avatar need to be the same as the protective skin of chameleons and squids, which will make adaptive adjustments according to the situation and has the ability to regenerate soon. However, unlike the conservative strategies of chameleons or squids, the new protective textures are not intended to blend in with the environment, but rather serve as a stunning visual reminder: a striking reflection on cyber temperament. 

This means the recovery of virtual trauma can be quick, stumbling, enlightening, and has the possibilities for reconstitution including the utopian dream of building a monstrous world without gender, accompanied by the sampling in the perspective of biology.
